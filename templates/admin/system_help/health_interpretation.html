Client Health: Reading the Badges

The Client Health overview shows one row per client company with a status badge that summarizes how engaged they are. The badge logic lives in utils/health_metrics.py: _compute_status().

Badge meanings

Badge Trigger What to do
🔴 critical Overdue invoice OR ≥5 HTTP errors in last 7 days.
Wins over every other status. 		Same-day action. For overdue invoice: contact accounting → confirm payment, then mark paid in /admin/system/invoices. For error spike: check Developer Dashboard for the failing endpoint.
🟠 dormant No login in 30+ days. High churn risk. Sales should call within a week — find out if they've stopped or are just busy. Often the trigger for a recovery offer.
🟡 quiet No login in 7–30 days. Light usage. Email a check-in. Common after staff turnover at the client; might need re-onboarding.
🟢 healthy Logged in within 7 days. Normal. No action needed.
🔵 new Never logged in. Just signed up. If >7 days since contract started without first login, contact them — onboarding may be stuck.

Why critical can override healthy

A client could be logging in daily AND have an overdue invoice — they're using the system but not paying for it. The 🔴 wins so it's impossible to overlook.

Practical: when triaging the overview, sort by status first (critical → dormant → quiet → ...). Use the filter pills at the top of the page to focus on one status at a time.

Why tier matters even on a "healthy" row

The tier badge (Entry / Standard / Premium) is shown alongside the status because same status = different urgency depending on revenue. A 🟠 dormant Premium customer (¥120k/month at risk) is roughly 8× more important than a dormant Entry customer (¥15k/month). Use it to prioritize who to contact first.

Internal accounts (Kurajika's own)

By default the overview hides Kurajika's own company (and any company marked is_internal = TRUE). Use the "Include internal" toggle next to the filter pills when smoke-testing or checking your own engagement. Internal rows render with a yellow tint and a 🏠 marker.

Drill-down: company & store detail

Click Detail → on any row to see:

  • 4 KPI cards (active users, logins, purchases, stock counts — last 30d)
  • 6-month bar chart trend
  • Feature usage table (which gated features they actually touched)
  • Per-user activity (last login, action count)
  • Per-store breakdown (with further drill-down)
  • 🖨 Print button — uses CSS @media print; just press OS Print → "Save as PDF" to email a snapshot to a client.

What's NOT shown (yet)

  • Cross-tenant anonymized benchmarks ("your food cost is 2.3% better than peers") — phase 2.
  • Auto-emailed monthly health PDF — needs an email provider; deferred.
  • "You saved X hours" calculation — not yet computed.
  • Period selector (currently fixed at 30d).
